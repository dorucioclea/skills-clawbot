# NEET Exam Configuration

## Exam Structure (2024+)

### Subject Distribution
| Subject | Questions | Marks | Duration Share |
|---------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| Physics | 45 | 180 | ~55 min |
| Chemistry | 45 | 180 | ~55 min |
| Botany | 45 | 180 | ~55 min |
| Zoology | 45 | 180 | ~55 min |
| **Total** | **180** | **720** | **200 min** |

### Marking Scheme
- Correct answer: +4 marks
- Wrong answer: -1 mark
- Unattempted: 0 marks
- No partial marking

### Section Pattern (per subject)
- Section A: 35 questions (all mandatory)
- Section B: 15 questions (attempt any 10)
- Total attempted: 45 per subject, 180 total

### State Domicile Benefits
- 85% seats in government colleges reserved for state domicile
- 15% All India Quota open to all
- Some states have institutional preference

## Counselling Process

### AIQ Counselling (MCC)
1. Registration and payment
2. Choice filling (colleges + courses)
3. Seat allotment (4 rounds typically)
4. Reporting to college
5. Upgradation in subsequent rounds

### State Counselling
- Separate registration required
- Runs parallel to AIQ rounds
- Different counselling authorities per state
- Can participate in both AIQ and State

## Eligibility Criteria

- Age: 17-25 years (relaxation for reserved categories)
- Education: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology
- Minimum marks: 50% aggregate (40% for OBC/SC/ST)
- Attempts: No limit (age cap applies)
